What is Big-O Notation?

BigO notation is the measurement and efficiency of code with `Time Complexity` and `Space Complexity`. Time complexity is measured in operatins, while space is measured in memory.

There are three different measurements you may come across as a software engineer:

Ω - Omega is the most efficient and is the best case scenario.

Θ - Theta has an average efficiency and is the average case scenario.

O - Omicron is the worst case scenario. It is what we look for when building systems and code blocks. If we can tackle the worse case scenario, we can increase the quality of our code. The better the code, the better all-around quality of technology.

Essentially, BigO notation is a way to measure the efficiency of an algorithm in its worse case scenario.

In this walkthrough, I will be focusing on the basics of BigO Time Complexity. I will be utilizing the Python Programming Language in my examples, which means I am going to assume you have standard knowledge of Python.

Though you may be able to follow along if you do not have that standard knowledge, it will be harder to visualize what I am explaining. Unless you're proficient in another programming language, then you may still be able to follow along.
